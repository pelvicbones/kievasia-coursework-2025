Ocean pollution is out of control !!!

Over 8 million tons of plastic enter the oceans annually, harming marine life and ecosystems

Tens of thousands of animals, including turtles, dolphins, and seabirds, die each year from :

1. Becoming ensnared in plastic debris

2. Ingesting plastic

Oil spills, such as the Deepwater Horizon disaster, cause lasting damage to marine habitats. These spills have long-term effects on countless species.


Climate Change is Destroying Marine Ecosystems !!

Oceans absorb 90% of the excess heat generated by Earth's warming climate.

This results in rising water temperatures that threaten marine biodiversity.

Coral reefs are diminishing due to coral bleaching caused by warming seas:

1. Over half of coral reefs have vanished in the past 30 years.

1. Erodes the shells of delicate marine species like oysters and plankton.

2. Disrupts the ocean food web, impacting marine ecosystems.


Emptying the Oceans

90% of global fish stocks are either overfished or depleted.

Non-target species like sharks, dolphins, and sea turtles are unintentionally caught, resulting in significant deaths

Scientists predict global fish populations could collapse by 2050 if current trends continue, potentially leaving oceans desolate.


Reduce plastic waste

Say NO to single-use plastics (straws, bags, bottles).

Use reusable alternatives (bottles, shopping bags, metal straws).

Participate in beach and river cleanups to prevent plastic from reaching the ocean.


Support Sustainable Seafood Choices

Use seafood like Seafood Watch to choose fish from sustainable sources.

Avoid eating overfished species like bluefin tuna and shark.

Support local and eco-friendly fisheries that use responsible fishing methods.


Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

Use public transport, bike, or walk instead of driving whenever possible.

Switch to renewable energy sources (solar, wind) if available.

Reduce energy use at home by turning off lights, using energy-efficient appliances, and cutting down on unnecessary waste.
